The Estate

La Villa

Our Story

Born in the
13th century,
alive today

Villa La Tartaruga stands as one of the finest examples of Emilian rural architecture — a privately owned manor whose earliest records date to the 13th century, with significant expansions during the 18th century that gave the estate its current form: soaring arched ceilings, generous room proportions and an air of quiet authority.

The villa was acquired and lovingly restored by its current owners, who transformed it from a working farmhouse into a refined locanda without erasing a single fragment of its historic soul. Authenticity was the guiding principle: original plasterwork, terracotta flooring and exposed stone have been preserved and celebrated rather than concealed.

"We did not want to create a hotel. We wanted to open our home."

Today Villa La Tartaruga operates as a small-scale luxury bed & breakfast and event venue, managed personally by the owner couple. Guests are welcomed not as clients but as honoured visitors — offered breakfast prepared from local Emilian produce, guidance on the region's finest restaurants and museums, and an unhurried sense of space that larger properties simply cannot replicate.

The estate is surrounded by a large private garden with centuries-old trees, a lawn ideal for outdoor ceremonies and barbecue evenings, and a series of intimate outdoor terraces where the Emilian light falls in a way that makes even the simplest hour feel remarkable.

Heart of Motor Valley
Strategic Position

Between
Bologna &
Modena

Via Garzolé 41–43, Rastellino, Castelfranco Emilia (MO). Situated in the legendary Emilian countryside, the estate occupies one of northern Italy's most coveted positions — the heart of Motor Valley.

For automotive professionals visiting Lamborghini, Ferrari, Ducati or Pagani, Villa La Tartaruga provides an unrivalled base: peaceful, private, and minutes from the factory gates. For leisure travellers, it is the perfect countryside retreat from which to explore two of Italy's finest cities.
